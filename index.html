FAQ - Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Bangladesh tourism, travel planning, and using this platform.

What is the best time to visit Bangladesh?

The best time to visit Bangladesh is during winter (November to February) when the weather is pleasant and dry. Temperatures range from 12°C to 25°C, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Do I need a visa to visit Bangladesh?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Bangladesh. Visas can be obtained from Bangladeshi embassies or consulates abroad. Some countries are eligible for visa on arrival. Check with your local embassy for the latest requirements.

What are the must-visit destinations in Bangladesh?

Top destinations include Cox's Bazar (world's longest natural beach), Sundarbans (largest mangrove forest), Sylhet (tea gardens), Rangamati and Bandarban (hills and lakes), and historical sites like Lalbagh Fort and Ahsan Manzil in Dhaka.

Is Bangladesh safe for tourists?

Bangladesh is generally safe for tourists. Like any destination, exercise normal precautions: avoid isolated areas at night, keep valuables secure, and stay informed about local conditions. The tourism infrastructure is improving steadily.

What currency is used in Bangladesh?

The currency is Bangladeshi Taka (BDT). Major cities have ATMs and currency exchange services. Credit cards are accepted in upscale hotels and restaurants, but cash is preferred in rural areas and small establishments.

How can I travel between cities in Bangladesh?

Options include domestic flights, trains, long-distance buses (both government and private), and ridesharing services. For remote areas like the Chittagong Hill Tracts, local transport like jeeps and boats may be necessary.

What languages are spoken in Bangladesh?

Bengali (Bangla) is the official language. English is widely spoken in urban areas, especially in tourism and business sectors. Learning a few Bengali phrases can enhance your travel experience.

What should I pack for a trip to Bangladesh?

Pack light, breathable clothing for the tropical climate, a light jacket for winter evenings, comfortable walking shoes, sun protection (hat, sunscreen), insect repellent, and any personal medications. For hill areas, bring warmer clothes.

Are there any cultural norms I should be aware of?

Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Remove shoes before entering homes or mosques. Public displays of affection are discouraged. During Ramadan, be respectful of fasting hours. Photography of sensitive areas (military installations, border areas) is prohibited.

Can I use mobile networks and internet in Bangladesh?

Yes, mobile coverage is extensive. Major operators include Grameenphone, Robi, Banglalink, and Teletalk. Tourist SIM cards are available at airports and city centers with proper documentation.

How can I book hotels and transport in Bangladesh?

You can use international platforms like Booking.com, or local sites like Shohoz, GoZayan, bdtickets, ShareTrip, and Jatri. Many hotels also accept direct bookings via phone or email.
